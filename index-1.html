Every few weeks I confront the urge to redesign my website. Part of it is because I'm so shamelessly online and have spread myself so thin (online persona wise) that attempts to unify my various selves end in long bouts of introspection, often to little avail. In lieu of a construction site GIF, here's me indulging in talking about myself — and things I'd like to address in some form or another:

  • One day I would like to tell people that I specialize in self-contained websites.
  • My design (and such) portfolio. Work from 2020 – Spring of 2022.
  • Expanding definitions of what a technology is, understanding human systems and bureaucracies (what tech obscures)
  • Interfaces of yore and the metaphors that shape them
  • Much faith in the potential of the book form. Also: book (and by extension, mobile tech) as jewelry/accessory.
  • Mythogram and the legitimacy/power of diagramming/charted, graphed things. The power of drawn text.
I'm interested in thinking of/using this website as a sandwichboard...
  1. the two-sided, hinged structure of a sandwichboard makes the person wearing it part of the advertisement. inexplicably this reflects my general attitude towards personal branding/self aware self promotion in graphic design.
  2. the format loosely reminds me of books, and how we might translate a reading experience in print to the web. it also feels like HTML (with its matching tags).
  3. i think there's a way to use the vernacular of advertising to both accomplish the primary purpose of this website as my portfolio, but also keep it personal (the personal/professional divide is what i struggle with here)
  4. i feel there's some expressive potential in terms of responsiveness if we take this sandwichboard metaphor to the extreme
