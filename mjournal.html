My Making Journal

-- Preface --

I am pursuing a Master’s degree in Computational Design — let this log serve as a diary for things I’ve made and ideas I’ve taken on.

What is computational design? I’ve come into this program thinking it had something to do with generative, programmatic design workflows... as I write this in early December 2024 at the close of the first semester, I’m starting to think of the degree as a mode in which I can work to engage with sociotechnical systems more meaningfully.

-- Asemic --

A shape grammar I developed from this thread about the relationship between writing and drawing. Has absurd beginnings from a clip of Nathan For You with the caricature artist, I was taken by the way east Asian scripts are generally represented by those who aren’t familiar with it: see wonton fonts and yellow peril propaganda.

I adapted it as a shakily composed entry for Radical Characters. There was something in it about this question of how can I take advantage of my ignorance?

With influences from asemic writing and botched tattoos of Chinese text, I suppose I am trying to ask, What does it mean when a script, a hand, a calligraphic style becomes adornment for Latin letterforms, positioned as a signifier of some sort of exoticism, edginess, or allure?

-- Data Entanglement --

A reflection on how the data I produce flows through my local internet of things: particularly interested in the materiality of the internet (specifically, my router data), I found a 2021 Federal Trade Commission report examining how six major internet service providers (mine included) “amass large pools of sensitive data, and that their uses of such data could lead to significant harms, particularly when consumers are classified by demographic characteristics.”

Rotating the knob/dial refreshes server locations between which data packets travel: this includes the hosting service, state, and local weather.

-- Kid Pix --

I met my first ever iMac during Demosplash and used Kid Pix to make a couple comics. The interface was so generous; it was exploratory without being condescending or overbearing, and was wonderfully, splendidly wacky. It reminded me of noodling around with HTML/CSS layouts during my fanfic writing/reading days — which peaked around middle school — as well as trying to develop Tumblr themes for the million and a half blogs I’d started and successively let collect dust.

-- Ye Olde Status Clokke --

A small status clock involving a microservo and a remix of my ever-stalled adaptation of Phoebe Anna Traquair’s writing hand.

The precedent for this was Isaac Blankensmith & Smooth Technology & co’s Paper Signals for Experiments with Google. I wanted to make something that likewise would be materially useful, and settled on this clock inspired by that of the metal status boards in some School of Art faculty/staff’s offices in the basements on campus — as well as the Weasley family clock, from my Potterhead days long ago.

This project really humbled me — this was the first time I had to scramble and concretely think through how I’d be attaching things — though I achieved it (so jankily...). I also overthought the code; the process would’ve been much smoother if I’d understood more clearly how the servo updated its position between runs.

I’d like to make some laser cut, refined version of this sometime in the future.
